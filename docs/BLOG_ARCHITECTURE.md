# BLOG ARCHITECTURE

## La Ultima Vigilia

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

The blog is not a casual content feed. It is an archive of warnings, briefings, doctrine, diagnosis, and formation.

Primary objectives:

1. Publish Spanish-first content around the mission.
2. Capture search traffic from men seeking faith, discipline, spiritual warfare, purpose, and identity.
3. Convert readers into email subscribers.
4. Prepare paths into El Remanente, courses, books, and the Watchman Universe archive.

---

## Editorial Principles

Every article must:

- Name a real battle.
- Expose the underlying spiritual or psychological pattern.
- Connect the issue to vigilance, discipline, faith, identity, purpose, or brotherhood.
- Give the reader a decision to make.
- Include a relevant email capture CTA.

The article should feel like:

- A battlefield briefing.
- A private confession.
- A warning.
- A diagnosis.
- A commander refusing excuses.

It should not feel like:

- Generic Christian inspiration.
- SEO filler.
- Lifestyle blogging.
- Soft self-help.
- Content for content's sake.

## Article Types

### Battle Diagnosis

Purpose:

- Expose a common hidden battle.
- Lead toward `La Guerra Silenciosa`.

Example:

```text
La guerra silenciosa contra tu atencion
```

### Enemy Briefing

Purpose:

- Explain one Force of Corruption.
- Identify warning signs and countermeasures.

Enemies:

- The Whisperer
- The Scarlet Queen
- The Usurper
- Leviathan

### Archetype Reflection

Purpose:

- Help readers recognize themselves in symbolic archetypes.
- Keep archetypes grounded as real masculine struggles.

Archetypes:

- The Watchman
- The Exile
- The Fire Warrior
- The Tempted Saint
- The Conqueror
- The Builder
- The Guardian
- The Mad Prophet

### Discipline Teaching

Purpose:

- Translate the Watchman Code into practical formation.

Examples:

- Prayer
- Study
- Physical strength
- Work
- Brotherhood
- Service
- Endurance
- Truth

### Mission Essay

Purpose:

- Explain the worldview, project, community, and future products.

## Canon Review Checklist

Before publishing, confirm:

- The article serves awakening, not entertainment.
- The writing is direct, masculine, and spiritually grounded.
- No archetype is treated as a superhero.
- No enemy is reduced to a shallow villain.
- Babylon is presented as symbolic corruption, not merely a city.
- Visuals follow dark cinematic realism.
- The reader leaves with conviction and a next step.
