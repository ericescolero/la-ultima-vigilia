# COMMUNITY ARCHITECTURE

## El Remanente

Version: 1.0

---

## Purpose

El Remanente is the community expression of La Ultima Vigilia.

It exists to gather men committed to:

- Vigilance
- Discipline
- Faith
- Purpose
- Truth
- Brotherhood
- Responsibility
- Resistance against Babylon

Members are not passive consumers. They are participants in the mission.

---

## Community Identity

El Remanente should feel like:

- A disciplined brotherhood.
- A formation environment.
- A watch post.
- A place of accountability.
- A refuge from spiritual sleep.

It should not feel like:

- A casual social network.
- A content fan club.
- A generic Discord.
- A motivational group.
- A place for endless talk without discipline.

---

## Member Journey

### Stage 1: Visitor

User encounters:

- Website
- Blog
- Social content
- Lead magnet

Goal:

- Email capture.
- Introduce mission.

### Stage 2: Subscriber

User receives:

- `La Guerra Silenciosa`
- Email sequence
- Mission content
- Enemy briefings

Goal:

- Move from awareness to commitment.

### Stage 3: Interested Remnant

User expresses interest in El Remanente.

Goal:

- Identify seriousness.
- Prepare for onboarding.

### Stage 4: Applicant or Waitlist Member

User completes future application or waitlist profile.

Goal:

- Clarify expectations.
- Prevent casual entry.

### Stage 5: Active Member

User enters community.

Goal:

- Practice vigilance, discipline, brotherhood, and responsibility.

### Stage 6: Leader

Trusted member helps guide others.

Goal:

- Steward culture and protect mission.

## Community Structure

### Public Layer

Open to visitors:

- El Remanente landing page.
- Mission explanation.
- Waitlist or interest form.
- Public creed excerpts.

### Subscriber Layer

Email-only:

- Introductory briefings.
- Community invitation.
- Formation previews.

### Member Layer

Private:

- Onboarding.
- Weekly rhythms.
- Discussion prompts.
- Brotherhood challenges.
- Member resources.
- Course access if bundled.

### Leadership Layer

Private:

- Moderation tools.
- Member review.
- Community health reports.
- Leadership briefings.

## Core Community Modules

### Onboarding

Required content:

- Mission
- The Watchman Code
- Oath of the Remnant
- Community expectations
- Daily disciplines
- How to participate
- What not to bring into the community

### Weekly Rhythm

Recommended weekly structure:

```text
Monday: Mission briefing
Tuesday: Discipline check
Wednesday: Scripture/study prompt
Thursday: Brotherhood accountability
Friday: Reflection and repentance
Saturday: Build/work challenge
Sunday: Silence, prayer, reset
```

### Brotherhood Challenges

Challenge types:

- 7-day vigilance challenge
- 14-day discipline challenge
- 30-day self-mastery challenge
- Prayer and study challenge
- Physical strength challenge
- Build more than you consume challenge

### Discussion Prompts

Prompt categories:

- What did you notice?
- Where did you compromise?
- What are you avoiding?
- What did you build?
- What must be corrected?
- Who are you protecting?

### Member Resources

Resource types:

- Devotionals
- Worksheets
- Audio briefings
- Course materials
- PDF guides
- Accountability templates

---

## Community Rules

Members must:

- Speak truthfully.
- Reject performative masculinity.
- Avoid endless debate.
- Respect the Christian foundation.
- Protect confidentiality.
- Practice accountability.
- Build more than they consume.
- Remain teachable.

Members must not:

- Turn the community into politics-first discussion.
- Use the space for self-promotion.
- Mock confession or weakness.
- Normalize lust, pride, passivity, or deception.
- Treat the mission as entertainment.

## Canon Review Checklist

Before launching any community feature, confirm:

- It reinforces the Watchman Code.
- It forms men, not just entertains them.
- It encourages responsibility, not dependency.
- It protects brotherhood and discipline.
- It remains Christian and mission-driven.
- It does not become a generic social platform.
